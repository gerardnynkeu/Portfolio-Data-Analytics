## Dataset Overview


The dataset contains weekly sales data from multiple Walmart stores across different time periods. It includes the following attributes:

Store: Unique identifier for each store.

Date: The week of sales recorded.

Weekly_Sales: The total revenue generated by the store in a given week.

Holiday_Flag: A binary indicator (1 = holiday week, 0 = non-holiday week).

Temperature: The average temperature during the recorded week.

Fuel_Price: The cost of fuel per gallon in the storeâ€™s region.

CPI (Consumer Price Index): An economic indicator representing inflation.

Unemployment: The regional unemployment rate.

## Limitations 

Despite the insights gained from this analysis, several limitations should be considered:

Data Completeness & Quality â€“ The dataset hat no missing values. Data cleaning techniques were applied, but incomplete records might introduce biases.

Feature Limitations â€“ The dataset primarily includes transactional and time-series data but lacks detailed customer demographics, marketing campaigns, or external economic factors that influence sales trends.

Holiday Influence Simplification â€“ The dataset flags holidays, but it does not account for varying regional effects, holiday-specific promotions, or seasonal factors beyond binary classification.

External Market Conditions â€“ Economic indicators, competitor pricing, and supply chain factors are not included, which limits a more comprehensive market analysis.

Time Scope Constraints â€“ The dataset spans a limited period, meaning long-term trends, shifts in consumer behavior, and emerging market patterns may not be fully captured.

Granularity of Data â€“ The dataset aggregates sales at a higher level, potentially masking store-specific or product-level insights that could enhance targeted decision-making.

By acknowledging these limitations, the analysis remains transparent, and future research directions can be explored to refine predictive modeling and business recommendations.
